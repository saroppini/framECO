Circumstancec back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://www.w3id.org/framECO#Circumstance

Circumstances or conditions under which the Experiencer experiences the emotion. Unlike Content it does not give the specific content of the emotion, but the Content can often be inferred from the Circumstances.
is in range of
hasCircumstance op
is disjoint with
Content c, Stimulus c

Contentc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://www.w3id.org/framECO#Content

Content is what the Experiencer's feelings or experiences are directed towards or based upon. The Content differs from a stimulus because the Content is not construed as being directly responsible for causing the emotion.
is in range of
hasContent op
is disjoint with

Emotionc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://www.w3id.org/framECO#Emotion

An emotion is an Affective State lived by an Experiencer which involves or is evoked by specific Circumstances. For this reason it is usually intense and short-lived.
is equivalent to
AffectiveState c and (hasCircumstance op some Circumstance c)
has super-classes
AffectiveState c
is in domain of
evokedBy op, hasCircumstance op, hasContent op, hasDegree op, hasExpressor op, hasManner op

mannerc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://www.w3id.org/framECO#Manner

Any description of the way in which the Experiencer experiences the Emotion, including secondary effects (e.g. "quietly", "loudly"), and general descriptions comparing events (e.g. "the same way"). Manner may also describe a state of the Experiencer that affects the details of the emotional experience.
is in range of
hasManner op

Physiological Processc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://www.w3id.org/framECO#PhysiologicalProcess

A Physiological Process is a Bodily Process which is an Expressor of an Experiencer's Emotion. It can be a change in the rate of breathing, muscle becoming tense or relaxing, etc.
has super-classes
bodily process c
